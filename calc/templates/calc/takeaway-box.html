Investment returns (IRR):

Your peak annualized return is % if you sell in year . The long run annualized return of the S&P500 is ~7-9%.

Value Levers:

Shows the effect on returns from:

Mortgage: If the yearly appreciation is higher than the mortgage interest rate, a mortgage increases returns as you capture the difference between the gain and the interest rate

Avoided rent: Typically the largest value driver and represents the value gained from the rent you save by owning

Tax shield: Tax breaks from the home interest and state/local ('SALT') tax deducations. The higher your income the higher the benefit
